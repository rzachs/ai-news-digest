Trend to Watch

AI is colliding with trust — in government contracts, rental listings, consumer hardware, and product marketing — and the backlash is starting to stick. Anthropic's government feud, Sony's bad camera photos, and fake apartment listings all share the same root cause: AI was deployed to impress rather than to reliably deliver, and real people got burned. The companies winning right now are the ones building AI into genuine utility (Omio, the elephant warning system) rather than bolting it on as a feature. This week, watch for how OpenAI's cybersecurity-focused GPT-5.5 is received — it's one of the first major moves to position AI as a trusted, specialized tool for high-stakes decisions, and its reception will tell you a lot about whether the market is ready to trust AI in serious contexts.
