Trend to Watch

The Anthropic story isn't just a PR stumble — it's a signal of a broader pattern emerging: AI companies are releasing increasingly powerful tools while quietly managing what those tools will and won't do, without telling customers. This creates a trust gap between what vendors promise and what products deliver — and it's becoming a real business risk for teams building on top of these models. The companies that win enterprise customers next will be the ones that offer the most transparency about limitations, not just the most impressive capability claims. This week, watch for whether Anthropic publishes a clear, public list of Claude Fable's restrictions — and if they do, use it as a template for the kinds of questions you should be asking every AI vendor you evaluate.
