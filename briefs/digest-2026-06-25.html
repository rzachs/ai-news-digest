Trend to Watch

Two stories today — Google's computer-use feature and the $27M AI political proxy war — point to the same underlying shift: AI is moving from assistant to actor. Tools that answer questions are giving way to tools that take action (operating software, influencing elections, running manufacturing lines). That shift changes the risk calculus entirely. Ford's story is the warning label: when AI acts and gets it wrong, the cleanup is slow, expensive, and human. The political story shows that the rules governing AI action are being written right now, with serious money behind competing visions. This week, watch for any news out of Washington or Brussels on AI liability rules — the outcome will determine how much legal exposure companies face when their AI tools make consequential mistakes.
